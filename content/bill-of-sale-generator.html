The moment money changes hands for a used car, a trailer, or a piece of equipment, two people walk away with very different memories of what just happened. A bill of sale freezes the facts in writing while everyone still agrees on them.

It will not transfer a title on its own, but it answers the questions that cause most after-the-fact disputes: who, what, how much, and on what day.

Seller and buyer names

Use full legal names, not nicknames or a business's informal trade name, and make them match the names that appear on any related title or ID. If a vehicle title reads "Robert" but the bill of sale says "Bob," a clerk at the DMV can reject the paperwork. When a company is the buyer or seller, write the registered entity name and note the role of the person signing (for example, "as owner" or "as authorized agent").

Both names matter because a bill of sale is, at its core, a record of a transfer between two specific parties. If a question ever arises about who actually sold the item, the document needs to point at exactly one seller and one buyer.

Description of the item

Be specific enough that the item could not be confused with anything else. For a vehicle, that means year, make, model, and the full vehicle identification number (VIN). For a trailer, boat, or piece of machinery, include the serial or hull number. For general goods, describe make, model, color, and condition. A vague line like "old truck" protects no one; "2014 Ford F-150, VIN 1FTFW1ET5EKE12345, silver, 142,300 miles" leaves nothing to argue about.

This is also where you record condition. Many private sales are made "as-is," meaning the buyer accepts the item in its current state. Stating that plainly, along with any known defects, sets expectations for both sides.

Sale price

Write the agreed amount in numbers, and consider spelling it out as well to prevent altered figures. If the item was a gift or sold for a nominal sum, say so directly, because some jurisdictions tax transfers based on the stated price or a fair-market valuation. Note whether the price was paid in full at signing or whether any balance remains.

Date, city, and state

The transaction date is often the single most useful field. It establishes when ownership and responsibility passed from seller to buyer, which can matter for everything from parking tickets to insurance to a registration deadline. The city and state record where the sale occurred, which determines which jurisdiction's rules apply.

  • Match all names to government-issued ID and titles.
  • Include every identifying number you can: VIN, serial, hull, or model.
  • State the condition and any "as-is" language clearly.
  • Print and sign two copies so each party keeps an original.
  • Common mistake: leaving the odometer reading off a vehicle sale.

Frequently asked questions

Does a bill of sale need to be notarized?

It depends on your state and the property type. A handful of states require a notarized bill of sale for a vehicle title transfer; many do not require notarization at all. Because this varies, confirm the rule with your local motor vehicle agency before finalizing.

Does this prove I own the item?

For titled property, no. The official title is the proof of ownership; the bill of sale is supporting evidence of the transaction. It documents that a transfer happened and on what terms, but it does not replace getting the title properly transferred into your name.

What does "sold as-is" actually protect?

An as-is clause signals that the buyer accepts the item in its present condition and that the seller makes no warranties beyond what is written down. It does not necessarily waive every protection a buyer might have under state consumer law, and it does not cover deliberate misrepresentation. If the scope of an as-is sale is important to you, ask a qualified professional.

Can I use one bill of sale for multiple items?

You can list several items in one document if they are sold together in a single transaction between the same parties. List each item separately with its own description so the record stays clear.
