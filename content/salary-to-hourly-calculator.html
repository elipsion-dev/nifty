One figure in, all four out

"$25 an hour is how much a year?" and "$60,000 a year is how much an hour?" are the same conversion run in opposite directions, and this tool does both from a single input. Enter an amount, tell it whether that amount is per hour, week, month, or year, and it returns the hourly, weekly, monthly, and annual equivalents together. That makes it easy to compare a salaried offer against an hourly one, or to sanity-check what a rate really adds up to over a year.

Why hours and weeks matter

A yearly salary hides how many hours it represents, and that's exactly where hourly-versus-salary comparisons go wrong. The standard assumption is 40 hours a week for 52 weeks — 2,080 hours a year — but part-time schedules, overtime-heavy jobs, and roles with unpaid time off all change the real hourly value. The calculator lets you set both hours per week and weeks per year, so the conversion reflects your actual working time rather than a generic assumption. Drop the weeks below 52 to model unpaid time off, and the annual figure adjusts accordingly.

Gross pay, not take-home

Every figure here is gross pay — before income tax, payroll tax, retirement contributions, health premiums, and other deductions. Take-home pay is meaningfully lower and depends on where you live, your filing situation, and your benefit elections, which no simple converter can know. Use these numbers to compare offers and understand rates on an equal footing; use a paycheck or a tax estimate for what actually lands in your account.

Common conversions people look up

$15, $20, $25, and $30 an hour to annual salary; $40,000, $50,000, $60,000, $75,000, and $100,000 a year to an hourly rate; a monthly salary to hourly for a contract; and a weekly rate to annual for seasonal work. Because the tool is fully bidirectional and reversible, any of these is the same three inputs — amount, period, and your working hours.

Frequently asked questions

How much is $25 an hour per year?

Enter 25, set the period to "hour," and keep 40 hours a week and 52 weeks a year: it comes to $52,000 gross annually. Adjust the hours or weeks to match your real schedule.

How do I convert an annual salary to an hourly rate?

Enter the salary, set the period to "year," and the tool divides by your annual hours (hours per week times weeks per year) to give the hourly equivalent, along with weekly and monthly figures.

Are these numbers before or after tax?

Before tax. All figures are gross pay. Actual take-home depends on taxes, deductions, and benefits specific to you and your location.
