Free Falling Letters typing game — no sign-up, no download, no catch. Letters drift down from the top of the screen, and your job is to type each one before it reaches the floor. It is the fastest, least boring way to learn where every key lives under your fingers.

The game starts gently and speeds up as your score climbs, but it never runs away from you. Miss three letters and the round ends — then you jump straight back in and try to beat your last score.

How to play

Press Start game and letters begin falling. Type the letter you see on your keyboard — there is no mouse, no Enter key, and no menu to fight with. Each letter you clear scores a point and gives a satisfying little pop. If several letters stack up, clear the lowest ones first, because those are closest to the floor. You have three lives, and each letter that lands costs one.

What it trains

Fast, accurate typing comes down to one thing: your fingers knowing where the keys are without your eyes checking. Falling Letters drills exactly that. Because a single letter appears at a time, you build a direct link between seeing a character and pressing the right key — the muscle memory that touch typing is made of. It is especially good for beginners learning the keyboard, and for anyone who wants to stop hunting and pecking.

Tips for a higher score

Keep your fingers resting on the home row (the a s d f and j k l ; keys) so every letter is a short reach away. Resist the urge to look down — trust your hands and let your eyes stay on the falling letters. Stay calm as the speed increases; a steady rhythm clears more letters than a panic. And do not worry about a bad round: the game reshuffles every time, so your next start is a fresh chance.

Frequently asked questions

Is it really free? Yes. There is no sign-up, no email, no download, and no paywall. Open the page and play.

Do you store what I type or my scores? No. The whole game runs in your browser, nothing is uploaded, and refreshing the page starts fresh. There is no account and no server keeping a record.

Is this good for learning to touch type? It is a great warm-up and key-awareness drill. For full touch-typing, pair it with our other typing games and the typing speed test to measure your progress.

Does it work on a phone? It works best with a physical keyboard. On a phone you can still play using the on-screen keyboard, though it is slower and more of a casual experience.
