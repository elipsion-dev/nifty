Social Security gives you a window, not one universal retirement date. You can usually claim retirement benefits as early as 62, receive an unreduced benefit at your full retirement age, or earn delayed credits by waiting—up to age 70. This tool turns those milestones into dates and applies the standard monthly adjustment to the benefit estimate you enter.

Use the monthly amount from your Social Security statement at full retirement age. The output is a comparison estimate, not a replacement for your personalized my Social Security record.

Read the result as a tradeoff, not a recommendation

Starting earlier usually means a smaller payment for more months. Starting later usually means a larger payment for fewer initial months. Health, work, savings, taxes, spousal benefits, and longevity expectations all affect that decision. The calculator intentionally shows the payment adjustment without declaring a “best” age.

How full retirement age is assigned

The Social Security Administration assigns full retirement age by birth year. It gradually rises from 65 to 67; people born in 1960 or later generally have a full retirement age of 67. SSA treats a January 1 birthday as belonging to the previous birth year for this calculation, which the tool accounts for.

What the monthly adjustment means

For a claim before full retirement age, the standard reduction is applied month by month: 5/9 of 1% for each of the first 36 early months and 5/12 of 1% for additional early months. For a claim after full retirement age, this tool models delayed credits at 2/3 of 1% per month, or 8% per full year, through age 70.

Limits that matter

This is a retirement-benefit estimate for the worker’s own record. It does not model the retirement earnings test, spouse or survivor coordination, disability conversions, government pensions, family maximums, Medicare enrollment, taxation, or a future change in law. Those details can materially change an actual payment.

Use the official record before filing

Compare this planning result with SSA’s full retirement age information and the estimates in your personal Social Security account. Filing is a separate decision made through SSA.

Frequently asked questions

Does waiting after age 70 increase the retirement benefit?

No delayed retirement credits accrue after 70, so this calculator caps that increase at age 70.

Why might my SSA estimate differ?

SSA uses your actual earnings record and program-specific rules. This tool only adjusts the full-retirement-age amount you supply.
