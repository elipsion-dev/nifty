The date that controls a date-certain nonimmigrant admission is usually the Admit Until Date on the most recent Form I-94—not the visa expiration date, passport expiration date, or the government form’s OMB date. This tool compares that I-94 date with today or a planned departure date.

Its output is intentionally labeled calendar difference. Immigration status and unlawful presence are legal conclusions affected by facts this browser cannot know.

First, read the correct field

Retrieve the most recent electronic record through the official CBP I-94 website. Confirm the name, class of admission, entry date, and Admit Until Date. The “expiration date” printed in the upper corner of a paper form can refer to the form edition and does not set the traveler’s authorized stay.

A visa and an admission serve different jobs

A visa generally permits a traveler to seek admission at a port of entry. It can expire while the traveler remains in an authorized period of stay. Conversely, an unexpired visa does not extend an I-94 admission. The I-94 record documents the admission granted by CBP.

Why D/S cannot become a countdown

Some records show “D/S,” meaning duration of status, instead of a calendar date. That notation depends on maintaining the underlying student, exchange, or other qualifying status and following program rules. Selecting D/S makes this calculator stop because inventing an end date would be unsafe.

Pending filings and corrections

A timely extension or change-of-status filing, a corrected I-94, asylum-related rules, age, protected status, and other circumstances may affect the legal consequences of a printed date. Departure records can also be incomplete after some land exits. Preserve tickets, passport stamps, receipts, and other travel evidence when the electronic history is wrong.

If the comparison date is past the printed date

Do not use the number of days alone to decide whether to depart, file an application, or reenter. Obtain the I-94, every USCIS receipt or decision, and the travel history, then get case-specific advice promptly. Different consequences use different definitions and time calculations.

Frequently asked questions

Does a pending extension automatically change the I-94 online?

Not necessarily. A USCIS approval notice may include a replacement I-94, while CBP’s travel record may continue to show the admission issued at entry.

Does this tool send immigration information anywhere?

No. The dates are compared locally in the browser and are not submitted to this site.
