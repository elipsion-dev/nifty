How to play

Press Start game and balloons begin rising. Type the letter printed on a balloon to pop it. When several are on screen, pop the highest ones first, because those are closest to escaping. You have three lives, and each balloon that reaches the top costs one. There is no mouse — just type.

What it trains

Like our Falling Letters game, Balloon Pop builds the core skill of linking a letter you see to the key your finger presses, without looking down. The upward motion and the pick-the-highest strategy add a light layer of prioritising under pressure, which keeps your eyes scanning and your hands moving — a fun, low-stress way to make key locations automatic.

Tips for a higher score

Keep your hands on the home row so every letter is a short reach away. Watch the top of the screen, not the bottom, so you always know which balloon is about to escape. Stay calm as the pace picks up; a steady rhythm pops more balloons than a panic. And remember the board reshuffles every round, so a rough start never sinks the next one.

Frequently asked questions

Is it really free? Yes. No sign-up, no email, no download, and no paywall.

Do you store anything? No. The game runs entirely in your browser and nothing is uploaded. There is no account and no server keeping a record.

How is this different from Falling Letters? Same core skill, opposite direction: balloons rise instead of falling, and you clear the highest ones first. Playing both keeps key practice fresh.

Does it work on a phone? It plays best with a physical keyboard. On a phone you can use the on-screen keyboard for a casual game.

Important

Balloon Pop is a free typing game for practice and fun. It is a learning aid, not a formal typing certification, and nothing is saved or sent anywhere.
