Movers and labor

Professional movers usually bill by the hour for a crew of two or three. What actually drives the hours: the volume of stuff, how much is boxed and ready before they arrive, and the "long carry" and stair penalties — a third-floor walk-up with no elevator can double the time of an identical load on the ground floor. Before you trust any number, learn whether your quote is binding (the price is locked) or non-binding (an estimate that can climb on moving day). Non-binding quotes are where surprise charges live. Enter the hours you realistically expect and the hourly rate you've been quoted.

Truck rental

Going the DIY route makes the truck your main vehicle cost. Rates swing with truck size, company, season, and pickup location, but the line that ambushes people is the one-way drop fee: returning the truck in a different city often adds a substantial surcharge on top of the daily rate, sometimes more than the rental itself. Confirm whether your quote includes a mileage allowance or charges per mile, and reserve early — trucks sell out at month-end and during summer, the busiest moving window.

Distance and fuel cost per mile

For a self-move, fuel scales with distance and the truck's thirst, and a loaded box truck burns far more per mile than your car — often in the single-digit miles-per-gallon range. The cost-per-mile field captures that fuel cost; if your rental bills a per-mile fee, you can roll it into the same field. For long hauls, this line frequently rivals the rental base rate, so estimate it deliberately rather than waving it off.

Boxes and supplies

Packing materials pile up shockingly fast for a full home: boxes in several sizes, tape, bubble wrap, packing paper, markers, mattress bags, and furniture pads. You can slash this cost by sourcing free boxes from grocery and liquor stores or a local buy-nothing group, and renting reusable plastic crates is sometimes cheaper than buying cardboard for a big move. If you hire packers, that's a labor cost, not a supplies cost — keep it on the movers line.

Lodging

Any move of several hundred miles or more likely means a hotel night or two, and a driving day in a slow, heavy truck is shorter than a day in a car — plan fewer miles per day than you'd expect. Enter the expected lodging cost here. Road meals and incidentals can ride along in the other-costs field if you want them counted.

Other costs

This catch-all is for everything that doesn't slot elsewhere: setup fees, deposits, short-term storage, crew tips, cleaning, and replacing items that don't survive the trip. The tool also nudges you to add a buffer of roughly ten percent — because moves reliably surface a cost nobody listed, and a cushion keeps that from becoming a crisis.

Frequently asked questions

What's the cheapest way to move?

Almost always a self-move: a rented truck, free or low-cost boxes, and friends instead of a hired crew. The trade is your time, the physical effort, and the risk of damaged belongings or a strained back. For long distances, a portable moving container can split the difference — you pack, they drive — often landing between full-service movers and a DIY truck on cost.

How do long-distance movers price a move?

Local moves are nearly always hourly. Long-distance moves are typically priced by the weight of your shipment plus mileage, or as a flat fee. Because weight drives the bill, decluttering before a long move directly cuts the cost. Get two or three written quotes and confirm whether fuel, insurance valuation, and stair or elevator fees are baked in.

When is the cheapest time to move?

Demand peaks at month-end, on weekends, and across summer — that's when leases turn over and school is out. A weekday move in the middle of the month during fall, winter, or early spring usually means lower rates and far better availability for both crews and trucks.

Does my homeowners or renters insurance cover the move?

Sometimes partially, but a mover's basic "released value" coverage pays only a token amount per pound — pennies on the dollar for damaged goods. For valuable items, ask about full-value protection or check whether your own policy extends to belongings in transit. This estimator doesn't price coverage, so add it to the other-costs line if you buy it.
